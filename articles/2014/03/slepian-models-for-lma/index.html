Slepian models for LMA

Podgórski, Rychlik, Wallin

(submitted)

The article describes the concept of Slepian noise. A slepian models describes the behavior of a stochastic process (in time) crossing a certain level, $u$. The main contribution of the articles are method to simulate slepian for Laplace moving average models. That is models of the type $$ X(t) = \int f(t-s) d\Lambda(s), $$ where $\Lambda(s)$ is Laplace moving average.

We compare the Slepian Laplace model to the regular Gaussian version on data that micis cars driving on roads. The figure displays the upcrossing of the accelaration of a person given that drives on a Laplace moving average road. R ana matlab code for the Laplace crossing
